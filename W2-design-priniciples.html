Rule of Thirds

https://www.vans.com/

On March 16, 1966, in Anaheim, California. Brothers Paul and James Van Doren, opened up a small shoe store known as "The Van Doren Rubber Company". That opening day, they sold 12 pairs of shoes. Little did they know that their deck shoes, known simply as the "Authentic", would eventually be the sole of the worldwide company known as "VANS".
They would eventually find their niche in the blossoming California culture of Skateboarding. This new territory led them to design the first ever shoe made specifically for Skateboarding, the "Vans Sk8-Hi" in 1978.
We see that the placement of the shoe, follows the design principle known as "The Rule of Thirds". This design principle pulls the users eyes to the unique but iconic silhouette of the Sk8-Hi. What is not captured in the mobile screenshot, is the animation of the shoe changing colors. Even without the added attention grabber, because of the use of The Law of Thirds, customers see the shoe before they ever see the skateboarders in the background.

Contrast

https://www.urbanears.com/us/en/

Urbanears is an audio lifestyle brand located in Stockholm Sweden. Driven by progressive and minimalist design principles, Urbanears has created a eccentric brand of headphones and speakers. These aesthetically clean and user friendly devices stand out because of Urbanears unique selling proposition: COLOR.
Urbanear's flare for design is carried over in their web page layout. Here we see the use of the design principle called "Contrast". Contrast is used to show the distinct difference between the foreground and background colors, in order to enhance readability. In the screenshot, the similar warm maroon tones of the product, the model's hair, and the background color, contrast with the white text. In this case, the use of contrast not only makes the page readable, but highlights the core message of Urbanears. Color should be loud.

Repetition

https://www.ikea.com/us/en/

Ikea is synonymous with swedish design. Furniture focused on the ideals of simplistic minilmalism, uniformity, and eco friendly materials. These nordic tenets are infused into Ikea's whole business model. From their office chairs, to their bedroom sets, even down to the very layout of their retail stores.
The Ikea web page also echoes these aesthetics. Here we see the design principle know as "Repetition". Repetition is used to create a feeling of clarity, unity, and cohesiveness. In the webpage we see the pattern of: 1.) icon 2.) heading 3.) single sentence 4.) learn more link, repeated 4 times. As we look at this example, we can clearly see a swedish minimalistic approach using Repition to answer the user's questions. Now if only they will tell us what they put in those meatballs.
